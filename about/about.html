Rescuing British Wildlife

Dedicated to the rescue, rehabilitation and release of injured, sick and orphaned wild animals across the UK.

Since 2018, our small team has helped thousands of animals return to the wild — from hedgehogs and baby birds to foxes and deer.

Who We Are

Wildlives Rescue was founded in 2018 by a group of passionate volunteers who saw the growing need for professional wildlife care in our local area.

What We Do

Wildlives rescues, treats and hopefully goes on to release everything from Bats to Barn Owls, Hedgehogs to Herons.

Different enclosures, covering 6 acres of land, provide as natural an environment as possible for our patients.

We are a member of the European Wildlife Rehabilitation Association.

Our Charitable Objectives

Wildlives' charitable objectives are as follows:

  1. To provide treatment and care for sick, injured and orphaned wildlife with the ultimate aim of rehabilitating them back into the wild.
  2. To inform people about, and to create public awareness of, wildlife and all issues relating to the benefit of wildlife.

Wildlives will provide care and basic medical treatment for wildlife, and ensure that a bird or animal gets veterinary treatment where required.

With all animals brought into the Centre, the ultimate aim will be to release them back into the wild without taming them.

Euthanasia Policy

No animal with a chance of a good quality of life is ever put to sleep. Although the vast majority of our charges return successfully to the wild, some are left with ongoing disabilities and become permanent residents or are fostered to approved carers.

Sometimes, sadly, an animal has injuries that prohibit any acceptable quality of life and must be euthanised humanely.

The greatest reward is seeing healthy animals successfully released back into the wild.
